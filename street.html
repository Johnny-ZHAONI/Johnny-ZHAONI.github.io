On the Street

Xiangzi chooses work. The morning air is cold, but he pulls the rickshaw into the street. The city wakes up slowly: shop doors open, bicycles ring their bells, tram lines hum in the distance.

A passenger waves at him. It is a short ride, but it pays fairly. Xiangzi counts the coins in his hand and imagines them added to his small hidden savings. His dream feels one tiny step closer.

After some hours, the sky turns bright. The street becomes crowded. Suddenly, a group of soldiers walks past. They stop near the rickshaw stand.

Xiangzi feels something is wrong. Should he stay visible on the street, or quietly move away into a side alley?
