Evening

Evening falls over Beijing. Whether Xiangzi worked on the street or stayed inside, the day slips away, hour by hour.

If he worked, he has a few more coins in his pocket, but also more pain in his legs and back. If he stayed inside, his body is lighter, but his dream feels further away.

The city does not remember his choice. The tram still runs. The shops still close. The night still comes.

Tomorrow, he will have to choose again.

Start over — another morning

This is only one small fragment of Rickshaw Boy. In the full novel, Xiangzi’s choices are heavier, the losses deeper, and the city much harsher than one day can show.

Hypertext fiction inspired by Lao She’s Rickshaw Boy.
